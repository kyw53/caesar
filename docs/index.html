Divide et Impera

Caesar's Wars - Veni, Vidi, Vici!

Commentarii de Bello Gallico(Commentary on Gallic War) and Commentarii de Bello Civil(Commentary on Civil War), are the collection of notes and diaries that Julius Ceasar has compiled during those campaigns, and are considered one of the main primary sources on both the Conquest of Gaul and the Roman Civil War. In those accounts, Caesar describes the battles and intrigues that took place in the nine years he spent fighting and conquering the Gaul lands, as well as battling his opponents during the Civil War in the years that followed. Despite the historical inacuracies and the political character of those Commentaries, as they were used by Caesar to gather political support at home, they still provide a very fascinating and detailed account of the late Roman republic and how Caesar brought it to his knees.
